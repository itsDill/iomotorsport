🚨 Bangkok F1 Grand Prix 2028

The Bangkok F1 Grand Prix 2028 is scheduled for April 15-17, 2028. This historic event will be Thailand's first Formula 1 race, taking place on a revolutionary street circuit in Bangkok's historic district.

The weekend schedule includes:

  • Friday: Practice sessions and qualifying
  • Saturday: Sprint qualifying and sprint race
  • Sunday: Main Grand Prix race

Read our complete Bangkok F1 2028 guide

Bangkok F1 2028 tickets are expected to range from $180 to $8,000 depending on seating and hospitality level:

  • General Admission: $180-$400
  • Grandstand seats: $600-$1,500
  • Premium hospitality: $2,000-$5,000
  • VIP Paddock Club: $6,000-$8,000

Our packages include accommodation, transfers, and additional experiences at incredible value compared to European F1 races.

The Bangkok Street Circuit will wind through Bangkok's historic district, featuring:

  • Start/finish straight near the Grand Palace
  • Corners around major Buddhist temples
  • High-speed sections along the Chao Phraya River
  • Technical sections through traditional Bangkok neighborhoods

This unique circuit combines F1 racing with Thailand's cultural heritage, making it unlike any other Grand Prix venue.

✈️ Travel Planning

Visa requirements depend on your nationality:

  • Tourist Visa Exemption: Citizens of 64 countries can enter Thailand visa-free for 30-60 days
  • Visa on Arrival: Available for 23 countries for stays up to 15 days
  • Tourist Visa: Required for longer stays or certain nationalities

We provide visa guidance as part of our service and can assist with visa applications if needed.

Book early for the best experience and prices:

  • F1 Bangkok 2028: Pre-registration now open, official bookings start early 2027
  • MotoGP Thailand: 6-9 months in advance for best hotel availability
  • Other racing events: 3-6 months in advance

Early booking ensures:

  • Better hotel locations near circuits
  • Preferred seating options
  • Lower prices before demand increases
  • Time for visa processing if required

Thailand racing season (March-April) offers excellent weather:

  • Temperature: 28-35°C (82-95°F) - perfect for outdoor events
  • Humidity: Moderate to high (60-80%)
  • Rainfall: Minimal during dry season
  • Sunshine: 8-10 hours daily

What to pack: Light, breathable clothing, sun protection, comfortable shoes, and light rain jacket just in case.

🏁 Circuits & Events

Thailand has 8 world-class racing circuits:

  • Chang International Circuit (Buriram): FIA Grade 1, hosts MotoGP
  • Bira International Circuit (Pattaya): Historic venue, touring cars
  • Bangsaen Street Circuit (Chonburi): Famous street racing festival
  • Kaeng Krachan Circuit (Phetchaburi): Modern facility
  • Bonanza Khao Yai (Nakhon Ratchasima): Karting and club racing
