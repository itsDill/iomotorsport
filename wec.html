About WEC

The FIA World Endurance Championship represents the highest level of international endurance racing. Sanctioned by the FIA, WEC features the world's leading manufacturers competing in advanced Hybrid Hypercars, racing on legendary circuits from Sebring to Fuji, with the 24 Hours of Le Mans as the crown jewel of the series.

Racing Classes

Three classes compete simultaneously, each with unique regulations and challenges

Hypercar

The pinnacle of sports car racing. Factory teams from Toyota, Ferrari, Porsche, Peugeot, Cadillac, and more competing with hybrid prototypes exceeding 670hp.

  • LMH (Le Mans Hypercar) regulations
  • LMDh (Le Mans Daytona h) regulations
  • Hybrid powertrains
  • Top speeds over 330 km/h
  • Manufacturer works teams

LMP2

Spec prototype class using Gibson V8 engines. Pro-Am teams competing for overall and class victories in a highly competitive field.

  • Spec Gibson GK428 V8 engine
  • Four chassis manufacturers
  • Pro-Am driver lineups
  • Cost-controlled racing
  • Privateer teams

LMGT3

GT3-based category featuring the world's top sports cars from Aston Martin, Ferrari, Porsche, BMW, and more in Pro-Am competition.

  • GT3 technical regulations
  • Pro-Am driver lineups
  • Balance of Performance
  • Multiple manufacturers
  • Customer racing focus
