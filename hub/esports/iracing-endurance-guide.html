iRacing Endurance: Building Your Team for 24-Hour Events

A complete guide to organizing and competing in iRacing's special endurance events with your team.

iRacing Endurance Team Racing Guide

iRacing's special endurance events‚Äîthe 24 Hours of Daytona, 24 Hours of Le Mans, and Spa 24 Hours‚Äîrepresent the pinnacle of sim racing competition. Completing these races requires more than individual skill; it demands team coordination, strategic planning, and logistical execution.

Team Composition

For 24-hour events, most teams run with three to four drivers. Fewer than three creates unsustainable stint lengths; more than four reduces each driver's seat time and complicates logistics.

Driver Selection Criteria

  • Consistent pace: Look for drivers with minimal pace variation across long stints.
  • Low incident points: A clean driver finishing reliably beats a fast driver who crashes.
  • Time zone compatibility: Distribute your team across time zones if possible.
  • Communication skills: Clear radio communication prevents costly mistakes.

"In endurance racing, your fastest driver is often less valuable than your most consistent driver. We'd rather have someone a second slower who never makes mistakes."

‚Äî Experienced iRacing team manager

Pre-Event Preparation

Practice Schedule

Begin dedicated practice at least two weeks before the event. Focus on:

  • Individual qualifying pace and race pace
  • Night driving (essential for 24-hour events)
  • Tire management across long stints
  • Driver swap procedures
  • Fuel calculations for different conditions

Setup Development

Develop a setup that prioritizes stability over outright pace. Remember that your drivers will need to execute this setup at 3 AM after hours of racing. A forgiving car saves more time than an aggressive one that requires constant attention.

Race Day Logistics

Driver Rotation Schedule

Create a detailed rotation schedule before the race. Standard stints range from 1.5 to 2.5 hours, depending on driver availability and fatigue levels. Build in flexibility for unexpected circumstances.

Sample 24-hour rotation with 4 drivers:

  • Driver 1: Hours 0-2, 8-10, 16-18
  • Driver 2: Hours 2-4, 10-12, 18-20
  • Driver 3: Hours 4-6, 12-14, 20-22
  • Driver 4: Hours 6-8, 14-16, 22-24

Communication Setup

Use Discord or TeamSpeak for team communication. Essential channels include:

  • Driver channel: Current driver plus spotter
  • Strategy channel: Team manager and analysts
  • General channel: Off-duty drivers and support

Pit Stop Strategy

iRacing pit stops require coordination between the pit crew settings and driver execution. Pre-program your typical stops:

  • Standard stop: Fuel + tires + optional adjustments
  • Splash and dash: Fuel only for strategic advantages
  • Repair stop: Pre-calculated repair times for common damage

Managing the Night Stint

Night hours present unique challenges. Visibility is reduced, and driver fatigue peaks during the early morning. Strategies for success:

  • Assign your most consistent night drivers to 2-5 AM
  • Reduce target lap times slightly to account for conditions
  • Increase communication frequency during night stints
  • Have backup drivers ready in case of fatigue issues

Handling Incidents

Damage happens in 24-hour races. Having a plan prevents panic:

  • Minor damage: Calculate time lost vs. repair time‚Äîsometimes driving through is faster
  • Major damage: Immediately pit for repairs; continuing with heavy damage causes compound failures
  • Contact with other cars: Stay calm, don't retaliate, and report to stewards if necessary

Post-Race Analysis

Regardless of result, review the race as a team. Identify what worked, what didn't, and how to improve. The best endurance teams maintain documentation across events, building institutional knowledge that compounds over time.

Essential Tools

  • iRacing Live Timing: Real-time position and gap information
  • Crew Chief: AI spotter with strategic insights
  • Trading Paints: Custom liveries for team identity
  • RaceLab: Advanced telemetry and timing

The iRacing endurance community is welcoming to newcomers. Your first 24-hour race will be challenging, but the sense of achievement when you cross the finish line‚Äîregardless of position‚Äîis unmatched in sim racing.
