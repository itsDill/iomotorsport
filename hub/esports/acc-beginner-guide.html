ACC Sim Racing GT3 Beginner Guide

Assetto Corsa Competizione is widely regarded as the most realistic GT3 racing simulation available. The official game of the GT World Challenge, ACC offers incredible physics, stunning graphics, and a deep competitive scene. This guide will help you get started.

Why ACC?

ACC specializes exclusively in GT3 and GT4 racing. Rather than trying to simulate every form of motorsport, Kunos Simulazioni focused all their efforts on perfecting a single category. The result is unmatched authenticity‚Äîfrom tire models to aerodynamics to the sound of specific engines.

If GT3 racing appeals to you, no other sim comes close to ACC's level of immersion and accuracy.

Hardware Requirements

Before diving in, ensure your setup can handle ACC's demands:

  • Wheel: Any force feedback wheel works, though direct drive provides the best experience
  • Pedals: Load cell brakes significantly improve consistency
  • PC: ACC is demanding‚Äîmodern CPU and GPU recommended
  • VR: Supported but requires powerful hardware

"You can be competitive with entry-level equipment. Skill matters far more than expensive gear. Start with what you have and upgrade as your passion grows."

‚Äî ACC community advice

First Steps

1. Complete the Tutorial

ACC includes a comprehensive tutorial system. Don't skip it‚Äîthe driving model requires specific techniques that differ from arcade racing games.

2. Pick One Car

With over 30 cars available, choosing can feel overwhelming. We recommend starting with one of these beginner-friendly options:

  • McLaren 720S GT3: Forgiving handling, good visibility
  • Porsche 992 GT3 R: Excellent balance, progressive limit
  • Aston Martin Vantage GT3: Stable rear, predictable behavior

3. Learn One Track

Master a single circuit before expanding. Monza is recommended‚Äîlong straights for practicing top speed, chicanes for developing precision, and the famous Parabolica for building confidence.

Building Consistency

In sim racing, consistency beats outright pace. A driver who laps within 0.5 seconds every lap will beat someone who's occasionally faster but makes mistakes.

Practice routines for improvement:

  1. Set a baseline lap with no assists
  2. Aim for 50 laps within 1 second of your best
  3. Gradually push faster while maintaining consistency
  4. Only move to racing once you're confident in your racecraft

Setup Basics

ACC's default "aggressive" setups work well for learning. Once comfortable, explore setup adjustments:

  • Tire Pressures: Target 27.5-27.8 PSI when warm
  • Brake Bias: Move forward if rear locks, backward if front locks
  • TC/ABS: Start high (8-10) and reduce as confidence grows
  • Fuel: Calculate consumption and never over-fuel

Online Racing

ACC's built-in multiplayer includes a Safety Rating and Trust Rating system. Your behavior in races affects your ratings, which determine which servers you can join.

Tips for clean online racing:

  • Qualify within your ability‚Äîstarting 15th and finishing 12th beats crashing from 8th
  • Give space on first lap when everyone's close
  • Use your mirrors constantly
  • If someone's faster, let them through cleanly

Community Resources

The ACC community offers tremendous support for new drivers:

  • ACC Discord: Active community with tips and leagues
  • YouTube Tutorials: Aris.drives and Coach Dave Academy
  • Setup Sharing: Community setups available for all cars/tracks
  • Organized Leagues: From beginner-friendly to professional

The Journey

Getting fast in ACC takes time‚Äîhundreds of hours to become truly competitive. But the journey is rewarding. Each tenth of a second found, each clean race completed, each new track learned builds toward mastery.

Welcome to the world's best GT3 simulation. See you on track.
