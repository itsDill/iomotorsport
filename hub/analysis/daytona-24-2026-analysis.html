Daytona IMSA GTP Predictions

The Rolex 24 at Daytona kicks off the 2026 IMSA season with a stacked GTP field. Multiple manufacturers bring championship-capable entries to the historic superspeedway, setting up what promises to be an epic battle.

GTP Championship Contenders

The GTP class features the strongest manufacturer presence in recent memory:

  • Cadillac: V-Series.R continues development, now in its third season
  • Porsche: 963 has found consistency after early reliability woes
  • BMW: M Hybrid V8 hungry for breakthrough after near-misses
  • Acura: ARX-06 defending its status as Daytona specialist
  • Lamborghini: SC63 still developing but showing flashes of pace

Key Storylines

The Cadillac Drought

Despite being American motorsport's flagship program, Cadillac has struggled at Daytona. The V-Series.R has the speed‚Äîqualifying performances prove that‚Äîbut translating pace into 24-hour success has proven elusive. This year's effort is their most focused yet.

Porsche's Banking Mastery

The Porsche 963 excels in high-speed corners, making Daytona's banking theoretically favorable. Penske's operational excellence should minimize errors during the crucial night hours when races are often lost.

BMW's Moment?

The M Hybrid V8 has steadily improved throughout its development. Multiple pole positions suggest the pace exists. The question is whether BMW's endurance racing learning curve has reached the point where victory is possible.

Weather Factor

January in Florida brings unpredictable weather. The forecast suggests possible rain during Sunday morning‚Äîhistorically a period that has reshuffled Daytona results dramatically. Teams with wet-weather setup expertise could find significant advantages.

Our Predictions

After analyzing testing times, historical performance, and team form:

  1. Porsche Penske Motorsport: Experience and speed combine
  2. Cadillac Racing: Best chance yet for breakthrough
  3. Wayne Taylor Racing Acura: Never count out Daytona specialists

GTD Pro Battle

The GT3-based GTD Pro class promises door-to-door racing throughout. Corvette, Porsche, BMW, Mercedes-AMG, and Aston Martin all field strong entries. The class often provides the most entertaining racing as professional drivers push customer machinery to its limits.

What to Watch

Key moments to follow during the 24 hours:

  • Opening Hours: Aggression levels‚Äîwho settles vs. who overdrives
  • Sunset: Setup changes for night running
  • Dawn: Who survived the night in strong position
  • Final 2 Hours: Push for glory or manage to finish

The Rolex 24 sets the tone for the entire IMSA season. Victory at Daytona carries momentum that lasts through Sebring, Le Mans, and beyond. For every manufacturer in the paddock, this race matters enormously.
