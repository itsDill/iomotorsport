The GT3 Revolution in WEC

How customer racing machinery has transformed the World Endurance Championship's GT class.

GT3 WEC LMGT3 Customer Racing

The decision to replace GTE with GT3 machinery in the FIA WEC was controversial. Traditionalists mourned the loss of bespoke endurance racing cars. But the results speak for themselves‚ÄîLMGT3 has delivered the most competitive GT racing in WEC history.

A New Era of Diversity

The LMGT3 grid now features machinery from over a dozen manufacturers. Porsche, Ferrari, BMW, Mercedes-AMG, Aston Martin, McLaren, Lamborghini, Corvette‚Äîthe variety is unprecedented. Each race weekend sees different cars fighting for supremacy depending on BoP adjustments and circuit characteristics.

The Customer Racing Model

GT3's fundamental strength is its accessibility. Teams can purchase competitive machinery directly from manufacturers, with comprehensive support packages available. This has attracted new entrants to WEC who could never have afforded the development costs of GTE.

"We went from two or three competitive cars per race to fifteen or more fighting for podiums. The racing is incredible, and the costs are manageable."

‚Äî Team Principal, leading LMGT3 entry

Balance of Performance

The success of LMGT3 hinges on the Balance of Performance system. The FIA and ACO work continuously to ensure no single manufacturer dominates. Weight, power, and aerodynamic adjustments are made regularly based on data gathered from races and tests.

Critics argue BoP artificially limits competition, but the proof is in the results. Race-winning margins are consistently tight, and the championship fight remains open throughout the season.

Looking Forward

The GT3 revolution has proven that endurance racing can thrive with customer machinery. The model offers sustainability that GTE never could, ensuring manufacturers can justify their racing investments through car sales to private teams.

For fans, the result is spectacular multi-class racing at every WEC round. The GT class may no longer produce purpose-built prototypes, but it has never been more entertaining.
