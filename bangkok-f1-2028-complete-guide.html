üèÅ The Bangkok Street Circuit: A Racing Marvel

The Bangkok F1 circuit will be unlike anything Formula 1 has seen before. Designed by Hermann Tilke in collaboration with Thai architects, the 5.2-kilometer street circuit will showcase Bangkok's most iconic landmarks while providing thrilling racing action.

Circuit Route & Key Features

  • Start/Finish Straight: 800-meter straight along Rajadamnern Avenue, passing the Grand Palace
  • Royal Corner: 90-degree right-hander offering overtaking opportunities
  • Chao Phraya Chicane: Technical section alongside the river with elevation changes
  • Temple Complex: High-speed section passing Wat Pho and Wat Arun viewpoints
  • Khao San Straight: 600-meter DRS zone perfect for overtaking
  • Democracy Monument Turn: Challenging 180-degree hairpin

üîç Frequently Asked Questions About Bangkok F1 2028

When do Bangkok F1 2028 tickets go on sale?

Pre-registration opens in January 2026, with priority sales for Thai residents in March 2026. International general sales begin in May 2026. We recommend joining pre-registration lists immediately as this will be the most sought-after F1 ticket in history.

How much will Bangkok F1 tickets cost?

Estimated prices range from $180-250 for general admission to $5,000-8,000 for Paddock Club experiences. This makes Bangkok significantly more affordable than European F1 venues while offering comparable or superior experiences.

Where exactly will the Bangkok F1 circuit be located?

The 5.2km street circuit will wind through Bangkok's historic center, including Rajadamnern Avenue (start/finish), passing the Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Wat Arun, and Democracy Monument. It's the first F1 circuit to incorporate UNESCO World Heritage sites.

What's the best time to book accommodation for F1 weekend?

Book 12-18 months in advance for the best selection and rates. Hotels near the circuit will likely increase prices 300-500% during F1 weekend. Consider staying slightly outside Bangkok with good transport links for better value.

Do I need a visa to visit Thailand for F1?

Many countries receive 30-day visa exemption for tourism. Check Thailand's visa requirements for your nationality. US, UK, EU, Australian, and most developed countries get visa-free entry for short stays.

How does Bangkok F1 compare to other Asian F1 races?

Bangkok offers better value than Singapore, more cultural experiences than Japan, and a more spectacular street circuit than any current F1 venue. The combination of historic landmarks, street racing, and Thai hospitality makes it unique in the F1 calendar.

What's the weather like in Bangkok in April?

April is hot and humid (35-40¬∞C/95-104¬∞F) with occasional afternoon thunderstorms. The race will likely have a twilight start to avoid the hottest part of the day. Pack lightweight, breathable clothing and stay hydrated.

Can I combine F1 with other Thailand experiences?

Absolutely! Most visitors extend their stay to explore Bangkok's temples and culture, visit beach destinations like Phuket or Koh Samui, or experience other Thai racing events like MotoGP. Thailand offers incredible diversity for motorsports tourists.
