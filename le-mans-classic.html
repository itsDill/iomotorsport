Racing Grids by Era

Grid 1: 1923-1939

The Pioneering Years

The earliest Le Mans machines. Prewar sports and racing cars including Bentley, Alfa Romeo, Bugatti, and Lagonda. Open-wheel racers and grand touring cars from motorsport's dawn.

Iconic Cars: Bentley Speed Six, Bugatti Type 57, Alfa Romeo 8C 2300

Grid 2: 1949-1956

Postwar Renaissance

First postwar Le Mans era. Jaguar C-Type and D-Type, Ferrari 250 MM, Aston Martin DB3S. The birth of modern sports car racing with disc brakes and aerodynamics.

Iconic Cars: Jaguar D-Type, Mercedes 300 SL, Ferrari 250 MM

Grid 3: 1957-1961

Golden Age

Ferrari vs. everyone. The legendary 250 Testa Rossa battles Aston Martin DBR1, Jaguar E-Type, and Porsche RS60. Peak of front-engine sports racing cars.

Iconic Cars: Ferrari 250 TR, Aston Martin DBR1, Maserati Tipo 61

Grid 4: 1962-1965

GT Revolution

Ford GT40 vs. Ferrari 250 GTO. Shelby Cobra Daytona Coupes and Porsche 904. The era of homologation specials and manufacturer warfare.

Iconic Cars: Ferrari 250 GTO, Ford GT40, Shelby Cobra Daytona

Grid 5: 1966-1971

Prototype Era

The mighty prototypes arrive. Ford GT40 MkII and MkIV, Ferrari 512, Porsche 917, Matra 630. Big engines, wild aerodynamics, and speeds over 240 mph.

Iconic Cars: Porsche 917, Ford GT40 MkIV, Ferrari 512

Grid 6: 1972-1980

Turbo & Ground Effects

Porsche 935, BMW CSL, Lancia Beta Montecarlo. Turbocharged power and emerging ground effects. The transition from brute force to technical innovation.

Iconic Cars: Porsche 935, BMW 3.0 CSL, Renault Alpine A442

Grid 7: 1981-2000

Modern Classics (Special Grid)

Group C dominance: Porsche 956/962, Jaguar XJR-9, Mercedes C11. Then GT1 supercars: McLaren F1 GTR, Porsche 911 GT1, Mercedes CLK GTR. The pinnacle before modern era.

Iconic Cars: Porsche 962, McLaren F1 GTR, Jaguar XJR-9
