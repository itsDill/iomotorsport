About IMSA

The IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship represents the pinnacle of sports car racing in North America. Founded in 1969, IMSA features multiple classes of prototype and GT cars competing simultaneously, creating spectacular multi-class racing action at legendary venues like Daytona, Sebring, and Road Atlanta.

Racing Classes

IMSA features four distinct classes, each with unique regulations and competition, all racing together on track

GTP

Grand Touring Prototype

The top class featuring hybrid prototypes from Acura, BMW, Cadillac, and Porsche. These are the fastest cars in IMSA.

  • Hybrid power units
  • Over 670 horsepower
  • Top speeds exceeding 200 mph
  • Manufacturer works teams

LMP2

Le Mans Prototype 2

Spec chassis prototypes using Gibson V8 engines. A highly competitive class for privateer teams.

  • Spec Gibson GK428 engine
  • 600 horsepower
  • Pro-Am driver lineups
  • Championship pathway

GTD PRO

GT Daytona Professional

Professional GT3 racing featuring factory-backed teams in Ferrari, Porsche, BMW, and more.

  • GT3 regulations
  • Professional drivers only
  • Manufacturer support
  • Balance of Performance

GTD

GT Daytona

Pro-Am GT3 class with the largest and most competitive fields in IMSA racing.

  • GT3 regulations
  • Amateur drivers required
  • 20+ car grids
  • Customer racing focus
