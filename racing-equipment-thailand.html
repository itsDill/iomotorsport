Racing Equipment & Gear in Thailand

Complete guide to what to bring, what to buy locally, and where to find quality motorsports supplies in Thailand

üéí What to Bring from Home

Essential racing equipment that's best brought from your home country due to personal fit, familiarity, or availability.

Personal Safety Equipment:

  • Racing Helmet: Your own helmet ensures proper fit and familiarity
  • Racing Suit: Custom-fitted suits provide optimal protection and comfort
  • Racing Shoes: Personal shoes ensure proper pedal feel and comfort
  • Gloves: Racing gloves should be personally fitted for grip and dexterity
  • Underwear: Fireproof underwear in your preferred brand and fit
  • HANS Device: If you own one, bring it for consistency

Personal Tools & Accessories:

  • Tire Pressure Gauge: Your calibrated gauge for consistency
  • Setup Notebook: Your personal setup notes and data
  • Preferred Lubricants: Specific oils and fluids you trust
  • Electronics: Laptops, data acquisition systems, radios

üõí What to Buy Locally in Thailand

Racing equipment and supplies that are readily available and cost-effective to purchase in Thailand.

Commonly Available Items:

  • Spare Parts: Common mechanical components and consumables
  • Fluids: Engine oil, brake fluid, coolant, and cleaning supplies
  • Tires: Many international tire brands available locally
  • Basic Tools: Standard wrenches, sockets, and hand tools
  • Electrical Components: Fuses, bulbs, wiring, and connectors
  • Cleaning Supplies: Car wash products and detailing supplies

Cost Savings on Local Purchases:

  • Tools: 30-50% cheaper than European/US prices
  • Fluids: 20-40% savings on oils and lubricants
  • Electronics: Competitive prices on basic electrical components
  • Consumables: Significant savings on disposable items

  • Spark Plugs (set of 4): 1,200 - 3,000 THB

üöö Shipping & Logistics

Information about shipping equipment to Thailand for longer stays or major events.

Shipping Options:

  • Air Freight: 5-7 days, higher cost, good for urgent items
  • Sea Freight: 3-6 weeks, economical for large shipments
  • Express Courier: DHL, FedEx, UPS for small packages
  • Racing Transport: Specialized motorsports shipping companies

Customs Considerations:

  • Temporary Import: Use Carnet de Passage for expensive items
  • Personal Effects: Racing gear can often enter duty-free
  • Commercial Goods: New items may incur import duties
  • Documentation: Proper invoicing and declarations required
